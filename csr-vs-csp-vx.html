The restaurant credit — read carefully
🍽️
CSR's $300 dining credit isn't what it sounds like
It's not $300 at any restaurant. It's only valid at Exclusive Tables by Chase — a curated list of specific partner restaurants chosen by Chase. If your go-to spots aren't on the list, the credit is worth $0 to you.
It's split biannually: $150 per 6-month period. Miss a window and you lose that $150 — it doesn't roll over. This is the definition of use-it-or-lose-it.
It requires active management. You have to remember to book through the Exclusive Tables portal, at the right restaurants, within each 6-month window. That's the coupon-book problem with CSR's new credit structure.
CSP is actually better for dining in practice. The CSP earns 3x on all dining everywhere — any restaurant, no portals, no partner lists. On $9,000/year of dining (a real number), that's ~27,000 points vs. a restricted $300 that requires you to change where you eat.
Honest bottom line: count the restaurant credit only if you already eat at those specific restaurants regularly. Otherwise it's $0, not $300 — and it doesn't belong in the cost comparison at all.
